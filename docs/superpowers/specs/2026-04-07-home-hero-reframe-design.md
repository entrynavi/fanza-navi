# Homepage Hero Reframe Design

## Objective

Replace the homepage's explanation-first hero with a user-first entry point that feels friendlier, clearer, and more immediately useful.

## Problem

The current hero explains the site's intent instead of helping visitors act. That creates three issues:

- The first impression is stiff and self-conscious.
- The copy centers the operator's philosophy instead of the visitor's goal.
- The main visual area behaves like an explanation block, not a discovery entrance.

## Approved Direction

Rebuild the top of the homepage around three ideas:

1. Lead with what the visitor can do now.
2. Make the first three actions obvious: ranking, sale, and genre/review discovery.
3. Move reassurance into secondary UI instead of headline copy.

## Content Rules

- Do not say the site is "carefully designed", "prioritizes安心感", or "serves as an入口".
- Headline and body copy must speak in user terms, not operator terms.
- Copy should feel natural and short, without explaining the site's philosophy.
- Reassurance should appear as compact support text only: 18+ / affiliate disclosure / latest pricing on FANZA.

## UI Changes

- Replace the current abstract headline with a direct discovery headline.
- Replace the current explanatory paragraph with one short orientation sentence.
- Keep only two hero-level primary actions on the left: ranking and sale.
- Use the right-side panel as the explicit three-way chooser: ranking, sale, and genre.
- Demote reviews to a smaller support link instead of a competing primary entry.
- Keep reassurance as compact support chips under the primary CTAs.
- Remove any duplicated hero explanation cards that repeat the same route choices.

## Success Criteria

- The hero reads like a useful entrance, not a manifesto.
- A first-time visitor can tell where to click within a few seconds.
- The page feels calmer and more human without becoming bland.
